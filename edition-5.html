Jargon Buster

Scary 😨 Words: AI Agents

You’ve probably been hearing the word ‘agent’ thrown around a lot in AI circles lately. It sounds like something from a spy film, but it’s actually simpler than you think. So what is an AI agent, and how is it different from the chatbots you’ve been using?

Here’s an easy way to think about it:

A chatbot is like asking someone for directions. You ask a question, they give you an answer, and that’s it. You still have to do the driving yourself.

An AI agent is like hiring a taxi. You tell it where you want to go, and it figures out the route, drives you there, and handles all the turns and traffic along the way. It can take actions, not just give advice.

In practical terms, while ChatGPT or Copilot will write you a lesson plan if you ask for one, an AI agent could potentially research the topic, find relevant resources, draft the lesson plan, create the PowerPoint, and email it to your department — all from a single instruction.

We’re not quite there yet for everyday school use, but this is the direction AI is heading in 2026. Companies like Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic are all racing to build agents that can handle multi-step tasks autonomously.

Watch this space — it’s going to change how we interact with these tools very soon.

🔦 Tool Spotlight

NotebookLM

📓

NotebookLM

by Google

Google’s NotebookLM has been quietly becoming one of the most useful AI tools for educators — and it’s completely free.

So what is it? NotebookLM is an AI-powered research tool that works exclusively with documents you upload. Unlike ChatGPT or Copilot, it doesn’t pull information from the internet — it only knows what you give it. This means no hallucinations from random web sources (remember Edition 2?), and every answer comes with citations back to your actual documents.

You can upload PDFs, Google Docs, Slides, web links, YouTube videos, and more.

🎙️ Audio Overviews (The Star of the Show)

This is the feature that made NotebookLM go viral. Upload your documents and, at the click of a button, NotebookLM generates a podcast-style audio discussion where two remarkably natural-sounding AI hosts talk through the key points from your material. It takes just a few minutes to generate.

Imagine uploading your Year 10 revision notes and two minutes later having a professional-sounding podcast discussing all the key points. Students can download and listen to these on the bus, at home, or while walking the dog.

🎧 Deep Dive Two hosts explore your content in detail
⚡ The Brief Key takeaways in under two minutes
🔍 The Critique Constructive feedback on your material
💬 The Debate A formal back-and-forth on the topic

📊 Slide Decks & Presentations

NotebookLM can also generate presentation slide decks from your uploaded sources — and as of this month, you can now edit the slides directly inside NotebookLM. That means you can tweak the content and customise the deck without needing to export it first. It’s gone from ‘useful starting point’ to ‘genuinely usable lesson resource’ almost overnight. Is it Safe?

As of April 2025, NotebookLM is a Core Service in Google Workspace for Education. Same data protection as Google Docs and Drive. Your data is not used to train AI models. Just don’t upload student personal data — stick to curriculum content.

💰

Is it Free?

Yes! The free tier gives you 100 notebooks, 50 sources per notebook, 50 chat queries and 3 audio overviews per day. More than enough for any teacher.
